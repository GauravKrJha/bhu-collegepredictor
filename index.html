1

We build your merit per programme

BHU doesn't use one CUET total. Each programme's merit is the sum of its required subject tests. A B.A. (Hons) uses Language + General Aptitude (/500); a B.Sc. uses three science papers (/750); Shastri uses Sanskrit alone (/250).

2

We check real eligibility

Required CUET subjects, women-only colleges, the Mathematics rule for Economics, age limits and category-wise minimum percentage are all verified against the official 2026 eligibility document before a programme is ever shown.

3

We compare to last year's closing score

For every programme-college-category seat we use only the final counselling round it appeared in (Spot Round 2 → Spot Round 1 → Round 4 → … → Round 1). Opening scores and intermediate rounds are ignored.

4

We classify with a ±25 buffer

Your merit is compared to that closing score:

Safe · score ≥ cutoff + 25
Likely · within +25 of cutoff
Reach · up to 25 below cutoff

Predictions are guidance, not a guarantee. Cut-offs shift every year with applicant numbers and seat changes. Bachelor of Fine Arts and Performing Arts admit on a CUET + practical merit and have no published NTA cut-off, so they are not score-predicted here.
